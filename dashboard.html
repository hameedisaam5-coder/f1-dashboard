🟢 Status Banner
The coloured strip at the top of the page shows the current race condition.
● GREEN FLAG
Racing is underway normally. All gaps and strategies are live.
● SAFETY CAR
A physical Safety Car is on track. Gaps compress. Good pit window — the field bunches up.
● VIRTUAL SAFETY CAR
VSC deployed — cars must slow to a delta time. Similar pit opportunity to SC but gaps don't fully close.
● RED FLAG
Session suspended. Pit stops free for everyone — strategy resets.
⚡ Fastest Lap Bar
The purple strip below the status banner. Shows who holds the overall fastest lap of the race so far, their time, and on which lap it was set. Updates every 5 seconds.

🏁 Strategy Hub
Auto-generated strategy insights. All calculated from live lap data — no manual input needed.
🧠 Race Situation Cards
Auto-generated bullet insights. Refreshes every 5 seconds. Examples: leader gap, who has the fastest pace on track, overdue tyres, active undercut threats, and compound strategy split in the top 5.
🚦 Pit Windows
A card per driver showing how close they are to the typical tyre limit for their compound.
StatusMeaning
🔵 FRESHTyre below 50% of its typical life. No urgency.
🟡 OPENING50–75% of typical life. Pit window is opening — worth monitoring.
🟢 PIT NOW75–100% of typical life. Optimal pit window — pitting here is strategically correct.
🔴 OVERDUEPast typical life. Risk of tyre failure or heavy pace loss. Pit immediately.
📊 Tyre Stint Timeline (Gantt)
A lap-by-lap bar chart showing every tyre stint for each driver. The letter inside each bar (S/M/H) is the compound. The white dashed vertical line marks the current lap. Compare stint lengths to understand each team's strategy.
⚡ Undercut Threat Scanner
Compares each driver to the car directly ahead to assess whether pitting early (undercutting) could gain them a position.
VerdictMeaning
⚔️ Battle – HoldGap is under 1.5s. Too close to pit — hold position and race on track.
🔴 Undercut NowGap 1.5–5s and high degradation. Pitting now could jump the car ahead during the stop.
🟡 PossibleGap 5–15s with moderate degradation. Worth watching — conditions could change.
🟢 SafeNo immediate undercut threat. Gap is comfortable or degradation is low.
🔁 Overcut Analysis
The opposite of an undercut. Shows whether a driver currently ahead can stay out while the car behind pits, and still come out in front by the time they also stop — because the car behind degrades badly. "Laps to Cover" estimates how many laps the behind car needs to close the gap on fresh tyres.
VerdictMeaning
🟢 Strong – Stay OutThe car behind degrades fast and the gap is under 10s. Staying out is the better strategy.
🟡 Marginal – MonitorOvercut might work but it's close. Monitor lap-by-lap.
🔵 Not viableNot enough degradation difference or gap is too large. No overcut advantage.

A deeper look at raw car performance, independent of track position or pit strategy.

Ranks drivers by average lap time over their last 3 laps — regardless of position. This tells you who has the quickest car right now, not who is leading on track.
ColumnMeaning

📉 Tyre Degradation Monitor
Sorted by degradation rate — drivers at the top are losing the most time per lap due to tyre wear.
ColumnMeaning
DEG RATESeconds per lap lost due to tyre wear. Calculated via linear regression on the last 8 laps. Colour: 🔴 >0.30s (heavy), 🟡 0.10–0.30s (moderate), 🟢 0–0.10s (low), 💙 negative (improving).
WINDOWSame pit window status as the Strategy Hub cards.

⚡ Fastest Sector Times
Shows each driver's personal best and last-lap times for all three sectors, plus a Theoretical Best lap.
IndicatorMeaning
Purple cellThis driver set the overall fastest time in this sector across the entire session.
BESTThe driver matched their personal session best on their last lap — they're at peak performance in this sector.
S1 / S2 / S3 LASTSector time on their most recently completed lap (greyed). Compare to BEST to see if they're improving or dropping off.
THEORETICALSum of S1 + S2 + S3 personal bests. This is the fastest possible lap this driver could theoretically do if they strung together all their best sectors in one lap.
🏎️ Tyre Compounds
Different compounds offer different trade-offs between speed and durability.
SOFT
Fastest compound but wears quickest. Used for qualifying pace or undercut attempts. Typical life ~22 laps.
MEDIUM
Balanced compound. Solid race pace with better durability. Typical life ~38 laps.
HARD
Slowest but most durable. Used for long stints. Typical life ~52 laps.
INTER
Intermediate — for damp or light wet conditions. Not suited for a dry track.
WET
Full wet tyre for heavy rain. Maximum grip on standing water.
📡 How the Data Works
Update Frequency
Dashboard fetches new data every 5 seconds. The fetcher also refreshes every 5 seconds. Total lag from track to screen is typically 15–40 seconds during a live race.
Data Source
Powered by FastF1, which connects to the official Formula 1 timing feed. The same data used by broadcast graphics.
Gap Calculation
Gaps are calculated from cumulative race time (sum of all lap times). This approximates the real on-track gap. Minor differences from broadcast may occur on lapped traffic.
Degradation Model
Uses linear regression on the last 8 laps. Needs at least 4 laps of data to appear. Early in a stint, the value may fluctuate.
